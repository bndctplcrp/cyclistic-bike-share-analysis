# Act Phase — Strategic Recommendations & Business Application

## Final Conclusion

The analysis identified clear behavioral differences between annual members and casual riders:

- Members ride more frequently.
- Members take shorter trips, primarily during weekdays.
- Casual riders take longer trips, concentrated on weekends.
- Casual usage is highly seasonal, peaking in summer.
- Members demonstrate more stable, year-round usage.

These patterns indicate that members primarily use Cyclistic for commuting or routine transportation, while casual riders use the service for leisure and recreational purposes.

---

## Business Implication

Annual memberships generate more consistent and recurring revenue. Therefore, converting high-engagement casual riders into annual members represents a strategic growth opportunity.

The behavioral differences identified in this analysis provide a data-driven foundation for targeted marketing initiatives.

---

## Top Three Recommendations

### 1. Target High-Engagement Weekend Riders

Casual riders peak on weekends and take longer trips.

Recommendation:
- Launch targeted membership promotions during peak weekend periods.
- Display in-app upgrade offers after long-duration weekend rides.
- Offer limited-time conversion discounts during summer months.

Objective:
Convert recreational riders with high engagement into annual members.

---

### 2. Promote Commuter Value Proposition

Members show strong weekday usage patterns, consistent with commuting behavior.

Recommendation:
- Emphasize cost savings compared to repeated single-ride purchases.
- Promote convenience for daily transportation.
- Develop “Ride to Work” themed campaigns targeting frequent casual riders.

Objective:
Position membership as a practical and cost-effective commuting solution.

---

### 3. Launch Pre-Summer Conversion Campaigns

Casual rider volume increases significantly during warmer months.

Recommendation:
- Initiate marketing campaigns in early spring (April–May).
- Offer seasonal membership promotions before peak demand.
- Retarget frequent previous summer riders with personalized offers.

Objective:
Convert seasonal riders before peak riding season begins.

---

## Expected Business Impact

If a percentage of high-frequency casual riders convert to annual memberships:

- Revenue predictability will increase.
- Seasonal volatility will decrease.
- Customer lifetime value will improve.
- Marketing ROI will strengthen through targeted segmentation.

---

## Future Opportunities for Analysis

Additional data could enhance strategic decision-making:

- Promotional campaign performance metrics

- Survey data on rider motivations
- Digital engagement behavior

These insights would allow more precise segmentation and personalized marketing strategies.

---

## Final Statement

This analysis provides a structured, data-driven foundation for converting casual riders into annual members. The identified behavioral differences offer actionable direction for marketing strategy, supporting Cyclistic’s long-term membership growth objectives.
